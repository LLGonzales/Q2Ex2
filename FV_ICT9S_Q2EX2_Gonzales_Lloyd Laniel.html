Science Topics

  • Valence Electrons are located at the valence shell (the farthest shell) of an atom.
  • An atom would need 8 valence electrons to become stable but if it is a light atom, it only needs two electrons in the first energy level (the first shell) for it to be stable.
  • An element's group number in the periodic table will determine the amount of valence electrons that it has.
  • Impulse creates a change to the momentum of an object.
  • Impulse and force are directly proportional to each other.
  • The equation for impulse is j = ft (j - impulse, f - force, t - time).
  • Momentum is determined when an object's mass is multiplied to its velocity.
  • Momentum describes an object's "quantity of motion".
  • Higher momentum means that an object will be harder to stop while a lower momentum means that an object will be easier to stop.
